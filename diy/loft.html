Laying the insulation

  • Insulation should be level with the joists
    • Leave a 25mm gap around the bottom of the rafters for ventilation. Do not squash the insulation into place, as this reduces the material's ability to insulate.

  • Wires should go over the insulation
    • Recessed light covers (also called downlight loft caps) should be used wherever necessary to prevent a potential fire hazard.

Steps

  • Measure loft hatch
  • Decide on loft board
    • [Ref] Standard sizes are $2400 x 600$mm and $1220 x 320$mm, both of which are $18$mm thick.

    Could be the one.

  • Attach extra insulation layers to the floor or the roof?
  • Measure loft area
  • Decide on loft entry ladder and where in the loft it would fold on to once closed
    • May have to go for a more sturdy timber ladder that allows frequent usage. Some ladders come with a loft hatch door so may have to widen the hatch.

  • Noggins
    • For strengthening the joists. Apparently, if the joists are more than 2.6m, they need them.

  • Measure the distance between the centres of each joist
    • If the distance $\leq 450$mm, use board of 18mm thickness. If distance $\geq 450$ and $\leq 600$, use 22 mm board.

  • Plan the loft space
    • Create a 10mm gap between the rafters and the edges of the floor boards to allow for expansion. Try to plan for the gaps on either end of the floor space to be the same.

Loft legs or timber joists

Someone online has had $4'' \times 2''$ ceiling joists with $100mm$ of insulation. And laid $6'' \times 2''$ timbers on them to add extra $170mm$ of extra insulation.
  • $6'' \times 2'' \; \text{nominally} := 150 \times 47mm$
    • The finished dimensions: $145 \times 45mm$

  • $4'' \times 2'' \; \text{nominally} := 100 \times 47mm$
    • The finished dimensions: $95 \times 45mm$
