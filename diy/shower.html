Overview

This is the manual for the existing Triton Jade (IPX4?) shower unit. It has a power rating of 8.7-9.5kW. Good ref.

I like the Triton T80z fast fit range with its swivel fits meaning the electric cable and water entry points can come in from multiple directions. Fitting instructions. It is a thermostatic shower, meaning it keeps a steady temperature.

I could further increase the water pressure by using a low pressure shower head. It probably increases the pressure by allowing more air in? Electric showers may need their water tanks to be emptied quickly so perhaps an eco shower head may not be suitable with an electric shower. However some electric showers have eco modes. They tend to be more expensive though and I can't find a good enough video showing me the difference. This has some starting info.

I think the difference between an eco-head and a low-pressure head is the former deliberately shoots out water at a low pressure but makes it feel as if it's high pressure. Whereas the latter is used in cases where the pressure is already low.

Fuses and switches

The fuse at the consumer unit is rated at 40A. The shower fuse has the markings: B40, 5940s. This is a good example of such a switch. It has a Breaking Capacity rating of 6kA and a Rated Operational Current (ROC) of 40A.

The boldly written power rating for triton showers is for 240V. An equivalent 8.5kW for 240V is 7.5kW for 230V. So, $I = \frac{7500}{230} = 32.6A$. I need at least $I = \frac{8500}{230} = 36.9A$. With a higher power rating, a high flow rate can be maintained whilst not compromising on the temperature. Also, look for thermostatic options. An equivalent 9.5kW for 240V is 8.7kW for 230V - page 7. This is the shower we need.

Electric cable diameter

6mm cable can carry up to 9kW. On page 10, it says how to measure the diameter.

Entry points for water/electricity

Triton allows multiple entry points.

Shower pressure

One cannot have a water pump attached to the mains water supply. I'm guessing a lot of appliances/taps etc depend on the water pressure being standard like 1 bar and not any higher. In areas where the water pressure is low, a pump would be required but I'm guessing this would have to be disconnected from the mains. For example, water stored in a tank is pumped to the required pressure which is then fed into the shower.
