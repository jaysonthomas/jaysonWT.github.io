Research Interests

I started off my career in developing proof of concepts in the embedded sensors domain. From a technical standpoint, this involved evaluation of sensors, designing the electronics (in some cases this involved mechanical design as well) around it (both analog and digital), writing firmware that allowed smooth transfer of data, designing real-time algorithms for doing useful things with the data based on the purpose of the device and post data analysis.

Overtime, my desire to get back into core robotics has grown. My interest, these days, center around:

  • Design of control systems for underactuated systems
  • Simultaneous localisation and mapping in unstructured environments
  • Path planning in unstructured environments
  • Computer vision

Domains of current projects

Control

  • I go through the control for a 3D drone (with its nested loops) and a 2D robotic arm
  • At my job I have been working with a PID + adaptive Feedforward control scheme

Estimation

Computer vision

  • Fringe patterns
  • Convolution of kernels to find patterns
  • Lucas Kanade, temporal and spatial differences
  • Classification

Past projects

Advanced Fibreoptic Engineering Ltd.
An optical interrogator, helpful in sensing strain or temperature differences, is useful in structural health monitoring systems. In the below project, a Fiber Bragg Grating (FBG) laser was used. The laser had to be swept through a range of wavelengths (40k) per second. Based on the physical property of the medium (say strain), the received optical signal peaked at different wavelengths.
