Reflected Inertia

Background knowledge

  • Physics

    • Dynamics is the study of the effect that forces have on the motion of objects. Statics is the study of forces in the absence of changes in motion.

  • Control

    • First order form of system dynamics: $\dot{x}=f(x,u)$ where $x$ is the state vector, $u$ is the control input.

  • Pendulum specific

    • The simple pendulum is perhaps the simplest abstraction of a 'manipulator'.

    hekki

    $$x=[q,\dot{q}]^T$$
    $u=\tau$

Steps

We first write the system dynamics in a first-order form, $\dot{x}=f(x,u)$ where $x=[q,\dot{q}]^T$ is the state vector and $u=\tau$ is the input. What we are interested in is $\dot{x}$. I think $\dot{q}$ is part of the state vector $x$ because we can calculate $\ddot{q}$ directly from $q$.
