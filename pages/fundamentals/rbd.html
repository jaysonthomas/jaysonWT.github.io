Misc terms

  • Quasi-static assumption
    • At any point, a body accelerates, that means there is a force applied. That means, components of the body will have reaction forces to the applied force. But, if we assume the applied force is close to 0, then the body's acceleration is close to 0. The reaction forces will be minimal to the point we can ignore them. Great example of the spring here.

  • Newton's 2nd law
    • Mass, in physics, is a quantitative measure of inertia, a fundamental property of all matter. It is, in effect, the resistance that a body of matter offers to a change in its speed or position upon the application of a force. The greater the mass of a body, the smaller the change produced by an applied force.

    Force is an influence that can cause an object with mass to change its velocity i.e. to accelerate. Force can also be described intuitively as a push or a pull.

    Newton's second law is a quantitative description of the changes that a force can produce on the motion of a body. It states that the time rate of change of the momentum of a body is equal in both magnitude and direction to the 'influence' imposed on it.
