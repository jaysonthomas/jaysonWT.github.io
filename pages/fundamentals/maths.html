Differentiation

  • $\frac{d(L_1cos\theta_1)}{d\theta}$
    • $ = -L_1sin\theta_1\dot\theta_1$
    Further differentiation of which gives: $ = -L_1cos\theta_1\dot\theta_1^2 - L_1sin\theta_1\ddot\theta_1$
    Differentiation of $sin$ keeps the sign the same.

  • $\frac{d(cos(2x+b))}{dx}$
    • $= -2sin(2x+b) - sin(2x+b)\frac{d(b)}{dx} = -2sin(2x+b)$

Least squares

  • Subscript of $argmin$
    • $argmin_x f(x)$ is the value of $x$ for which $f(x)$ attains its minimum.

  • When multiplying with the transpose of a matrix
    • The order in which the multiplicands are placed before and after the multiplication is based on the dimensions of the matrices and how they change when transposed. I like this advice to first make sure to get the dimensions of all the vectors and matrices in place.

  • Derivative of a function containing $x^T$ and $x$ w.r.t. $x$
    • Great explanation.

  • Product rule
    • $$ \frac{d}{dx}[f(x)\cdot g(x)] = \frac{d}{dx}[f(x)]\cdot g(x) + f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[g(x)] $$

  • Why we set the derivative of $f(x)$ to 0 to solve for an unknown variable $x$
    • Ref1

  • What a derivative evaluated at a particular value looks like
    • $$\frac{\partial L}{\partial x}\Bigr|_{x=\hat{x}}$$

  • Matrix inverse
    • It's just the reciprocal. We don't have a division operation with matrices. To have an inverse, the matrix needs to satisfy the following 2 conditions:
    • Be square
      • That is have the same number of rows and columns.

    • The determinant should be a non-zero number
      • The determinant (which is just a number) tends to be 0 when there's no new information between the rows/columns of the matrix.

    A matrix for which an inverse does not exist is called a singular matrix. Good ref.
