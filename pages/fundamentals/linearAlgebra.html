Intro

[Ref] In 2d, a vector represents how to get from the tail of a line (say at the origin) to the head of the line. It represents the steps to take in a certain direction, say 2 steps along x and 1 step along Y. 2 operations that can be done using vectors - addition of 2 vectors and multiplication with a scalar. A scalar, just a number, is called a scalar because of its ability to scale or squish a vector when multiplied with a vector.
