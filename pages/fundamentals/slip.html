Ref. Extreme cases of wheel motion:
  • Perfect rolling
    • Distance travelled by wheel == Perimeter of the wheel. Contact region: contact between the road and the tyre will be a line. Relative movement between road and tyre will be 0. Slip is 0.

  • Pure sliding
    • The wheel moves but the perimeter of the wheel won't move at all. Slip is 100%.

  • Most common occurrence
    • The contact region of the tyre and road will always be on an area due to the weight acting on it. here if you observe the wheel moves less than its perimeter during a complete revolution this is because the relative motion between the tire and road won't be perfectly zero one portion of it will always be sliding

Musings

  • Why does a wheel just spin on the spot when a car is given a high acceleration whilst its velocity is still 0?
    • How much the tyre is able to stick to the ground briefly, depends on the material of the road and the tyre. We'll have to adjust the starting torque accordingly.

  • Relative movement between road and tyre
    • Let's say the wheel is initially stationary and we want it to move along a straight line. Mark the starting point:
    • On the road, $p_r$
    • On the tyre, $p_t$
    If $p_t$ moves with the tyre and $p_r$ moves too such that it is always in the contact line between the road and the tyre, then after 1 full revolution of the wheel $p_t$ and $p_r$ will coincide.
