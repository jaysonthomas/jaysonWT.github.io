Rotor dynamics

Forces

Each spinning rotor produces a thrust force upwards given by: $$\begin{align*} F &\propto \omega^2 \\ &= k_f \omega^2 \\ \end{align*}$$ The constant of proportionality, $k_f$ is the thrust coefficient. When the rotation speed of a rotor is doubled, the thrust increases by a factor of 4.

In addition to the same quantities as the lift constants beleow, the strength/magnitude of the thrust vector also depends on how fast the rotors spin. The direction is always perpendicular to the plane that the rotor spins in.

Moments

The torque/moment produced by a spinning rotor is proportional to $\omega^2$: $$\begin{align*} M &\propto \omega^2 \\ &= k_m \omega^2 \end{align*}$$ $k_m$ is the angular torque coefficient. $\omega$ represents the rotation rate of a rotor in $\frac{rad}{s}$. When $\omega = 2\pi$, the rotor does one rotation per second.

Lift constants

The actual values of the constants ($k_f, k_m$) depend on the:
  • Size and shape of the plates
  • Specific motors used
  • Density of the surrounding air they operate in
