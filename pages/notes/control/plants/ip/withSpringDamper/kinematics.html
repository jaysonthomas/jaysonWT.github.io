Pole: Linear velocity

Translational motion

The centre of mass of the pole moves at the same velocity as that of the cart, $\dot{x}$, in the $\hat{i}$ direction. $$ \newcommand{\wrt}[4]{^{\scriptsize{#1}\hspace{-#2em}}{#3}^{\scriptsize{#4}}} \wrt{W}{0.3}{v}{A} = \dot{x}\, \hat{i} $$

Rotational motion

A linear velocity due to rotation is given by $v = r\omega$ and is directed perpendicular to the radius (the length of the pole). $$\begin{align*} \newcommand{\avg}{\wrt{A}{0.1}{v}{G}} \newcommand{\arg}{\wrt{A}{0.5}{r}{G}} \newcommand{\wwg}{\wrt{A}{0.3}{\omega}{G}} \newcommand{\wwgTwo}{\wrt{W}{0.5}{\omega}{G}} \avg &= \,\avg\Bigr|_{\wwg=0} + \bigl(\,\wwgTwo \times \arg\,\bigr) \\ &= 0 + \bigl(\omega_z\,\hat{k} \times l\,\hat{d}\,\bigr) \\ &= l\dot\theta\,\hat{q} \end{align*}$$ $\avg\Bigr|_{\wwg=0}$ is the velocity of G as seen from A when G is not rotating. Since G does not have any linear motions, the term is $0$.

$^{\scriptsize{A}}r^{\scriptsize{G}}$ is the position vector between the 2 points.

Combining the 2 components

$$ \newcommand{\wvg}{\wrt{W}{0.3}{v}{G}} \wvg = \dot{x}\,\hat{i} + l\dot\theta\,\hat{q} $$

Pole: Linear acceleration

Refer here for derivatives of unit vectors. $$\begin{align*} \wrt{W}{0.5}{a}{G} &= \ddot{x}\,\hat{i} + l\ddot\theta\,\hat{q} + l\dot\theta \frac{d}{dt} \hat{q} \\ &= \ddot{x}\,\hat{i} + l\ddot\theta\,\hat{q} - l\dot\theta^2\,\hat{d} \\ \end{align*}$$
In terms of $\hat{d}$ and $\hat{q}$ unit vectors: $$ a_G = (\ddot{x}\,sin\theta - l\dot\theta^2)\,\hat{d} + (\ddot{x}\,cos\theta + l\ddot\theta)\,\hat{q} $$
