Cartpole with spring-damper attached: Kinematics

FBD

Pole: Linear velocity

Translational motion

The centre of mass of the pole moves at the same velocity as that of the cart, $\dot{x}$, in the $\hat{i}$ direction.

Rotational motion

A linear velocity due to rotation is given by $v = r\omega$ and is directed perpendicular to the radius, in this case, the length of the pole.

Combining the 2 components

$$ v_G = \dot{x}\,\hat{i} + l\dot\theta\,\hat{q} $$

Pole: Linear acceleration

Refer here for derivatives of unit vectors. $$\begin{align*} a_G &= \ddot{x}\,\hat{i} + l\ddot\theta\,\hat{q} + l\dot\theta \frac{d}{dt} \hat{q} \\ &= \ddot{x}\,\hat{i} + l\ddot\theta\,\hat{q} - l\dot\theta^2\,\hat{d} \\ \end{align*}$$ In terms of $\hat{d}$ and $\hat{q}$ unit vectors: $$ a_G = (\ddot{x}\,sin\theta - l\dot\theta^2)\,\hat{d} + (\ddot{x}\,cos\theta + l\ddot\theta)\,\hat{q} $$
