Cartpole with spring-damper attached

Setup

Cart

For any positive motion, the spring force resists and pulls back. For any positive velocity, the damper pulls back.

Friction and the reaction forces at the wheels are ignored.

Because of Newton's 3rd law, there are 2 forces acting on the cart at A that are equal and opposite to those acting on the pole at that point.

Pole

There are 2 possible forces $F_1, F_2$ acting on the pole at the point of rotation (attachment to the cart) at A. They are not drawn arbitrarily; their directions have been chosen. But, their directions are not known.
