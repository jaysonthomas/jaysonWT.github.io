Estimation: Usecase - Simple Kinematics

State to be estimated

Consider an object is moving from a starting position $p_0$, with initial velocity $v_0$ and acceleration $a$. The goal is to estimate $x$ given a position measurement $y$ at every time step. $$ x = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}p_0 \\ v_0 \\ a\end{bmatrix} $$ There are 3 unknowns - tricky!

Measurement model

From basic kinematics, the measurement equation/model is: $$ y(t) = p_0 + v_0t + \frac{1}{2}at^2 $$ If $\Delta{t}$ is the sampling period of the distance sensor such that $t=0, \Delta{t}, 2\Delta{t}, \cdots, k\Delta{t}$, the measurement equation is then: $$ y_k = y(k\Delta{t}) = \underbrace{ \begin{bmatrix}1 & k\Delta{t} & \frac{1}{2}(k\Delta{t})^2\end{bmatrix} }_{H_k} \underbrace{ \begin{bmatrix}p_0 \\ v_0 \\ a\end{bmatrix} }_{x} + v_k $$
