Estimation: Commonality in approaches

Measurement model

A measurement model/equation/function $H$:
  • Projects a state into measurement space or
  • Maps a state to a measurement

In the simplest form, a measurement model can look like: $$ y = Hx + v $$

Mathematical model of noise

It is not possible to know exactly how much noise gets added to each individual measurement. However, the distribution from which the noise is sampled can be modeled. Generally, the noise distribution is modeled as or assumed to be a zero-mean gaussian with some covariance $R$. To each measurement, a noise $v$, sampled from this gaussian $\mathcal{N}(0, R)$, is added. $$ v \sim \mathcal{N}(0, R) $$

Dimensions

  • $x$ is the unknown state vector to be estimated of size $n$
  • $y$ is the vector of $m$ noisy measurements - the estimate $\hat{x}$ becomes more accurate with more measurement samples.
