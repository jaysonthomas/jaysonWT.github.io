Estimation: Commonality in approaches

  • Measurement model $H$
    • A measurement model/equation/function:
    • Projects a state into measurement space or
    • Maps a state to a measurement

  • Mathematical model of noise $v$
    • Since measurements are imperfect, a model of the measurement noise $v$ is required. It is not possible to know exactly how much noise gets added to each individual measurement. However, the distribution from which the noise is sampled can be modeled. $$ v \sim N(0, R) $$ Generally, noise is modeled as a $0$ mean Gaussian with some covariance $R$. To each measurement, some zero mean Gaussian noise is added.

  • $m$ measurements $y$ are collected of true state $x$ of dimension $n$
