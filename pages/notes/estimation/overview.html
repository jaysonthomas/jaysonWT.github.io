Estimation: Approaches - Introduction

Assuming constant state

To estimate the state of a vehicle, for example, these approaches assume the vehicle is stationary.
  • Batch Least squares
    • It requires that measurements come in all at once. The measurement model $H$ is an $n \times m$ matrix, which maps a state vector of length $n$ to a measurement vector of $m$ individual measurements.

  • Recursive least squares
    • Having a batch of stored measurements is computationally intensive, since the amount of computational resources needed to solve the normal equations grow with the measurement vector size. With a recursive estimator, since measurements come in one at a time, $H$ will just be a vector, not a matrix. $H$ will map a state to a single measurement.

  • Non-linear leasts squares
    • The measurement model is some arbitrary function of the underlying state.

Adapting to changes in a state

To incorporate a vehicle's motion, for example, into the state estimation process, the Kalman filter and its variants called extended and unscented kalman filters can be used.

Bayes rule

Bayes rule is a method:
  • To integrate data over time
  • To come up with a distribution that is the best reflection of what the state might be, by considering multiple sources of information
Bayes rule relies on probability at its core to quantify uncertainty. There are other ways, such as confidence scores, fuzzy logic etc.
