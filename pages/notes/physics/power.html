Work, energy, power

Virtual work

[Ref] Work is the product of a force acting on an object times the distance that the object moves. When the displacement is infinitesimally small, the work is considered to be virtual.

Work-energy relationship

The work-energy theorem states that the work done on an object is equal to the change in its kinetic or potential energy. $$ \Delta{W} = \Delta{E} $$ This equation means that the work done on an object results in a change in its kinetic or potential energy. If positive work is done on an object, its kinetic energy increases, and if negative work is done, its kinetic energy decreases. When lifting an object against gravity, the work done is stored as potential energy in the object.

Law of conservation of energy

Energy can neither be created nor destroyed - only converted from one form to another.

Power

It gives an indication of how quickly work (linear or angular) is being done or how quickly kinetic energy (linear or angular) is changing/transferred.
  • Angular or rotational power
    • $$ P = \tau \cdot \omega $$ Angular velocity is measured in radians per second. Torque is measured in Newton meters.

  • Linear power
    • $$ P = force \cdot velocity $$
