Velocity dependent friction models

[Modern robotics, Drake, Mathworks]
Other factors may contribute to the friction at a joint, including the loading of the joint bearings, the time the joint has been at rest, the temperature, etc. Friction in a gearhead often increases as the gear ratio increases.

Viscous

This is a linear damping force proportional to the velocity which resists motion. Phenomenologically, this is due to a viscous (fluid) drag force produced by lubricants in either an arm-joint or wheels of a mobile robot. $$ \tau_{fric} = b_{viscous} \, \dot{\theta} $$

Coulomb

$\tau_{fric}$ can take any value in $[âˆ’b_{static} , b_{static}]$ at zero velocity. $$ \tau_{fric} = b_{static} \,sgn(\dot{\theta}) $$

Static + Viscous

$$ \tau_{fric} = b_{static} \, sgn(\dot{\theta}) + b_{viscous}\,\dot{\theta} $$

Static + Kinetic

$\tau_{fric} \geq |b_{static}|$ to initiate motion and then $\tau_{fric} = b_{kinetic} \, sgn(\dot{\theta})$ during motion, where $bstatic > bkinetic$.
