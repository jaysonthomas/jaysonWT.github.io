Moment of inertia

The SI unit of moment of inertia is kilogram meter squared (kgâ‹…m2).

Moment of inertia is the resistance of a body to changes in its rotational motion. It is a measure of how hard it is to rotate an object about an axis. The moment of inertia of an object depends on its mass and the distribution of that mass around the axis of rotation.

The moment of inertia of a point mass is equal to its mass multiplied by the square of its distance from the axis of rotation. The moment of inertia of a continuous body can be calculated by integrating the moment of inertia of all the point masses that make up the body.

Parallel axis theorem

[Ref1, Ref2]
Imagine an object on the screen with its z axis pointing towards me outside of the screen i.e. perpendicular to the object. The parallel axis theorem states that the moment of inertia $I$ about any axis parallel to (pointing towards me) and a distance $d$ away from an axis (pointing towards me) through the object's center of mass is given by: $$ I = I_{CM} + md^2 $$ The mass $m$ is the total mass of the object. $I_{CM}$ is the moment of inertia about the centre of mass.

Density

Density is defined as the mass of a unit volume of a material. It is an intensive property of a material, which means that it does not depend on the amount of material present. For example, a block of gold with a mass of 100 grams and a volume of 5 cubic centimeters has the same density as a block of gold with a mass of 1 kilogram and a volume of 100 cubic centimeters. It can be used to identify materials and to determine the amount of material in a given volume.
