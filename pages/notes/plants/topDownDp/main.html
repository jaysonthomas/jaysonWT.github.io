Top-down double pendulum

Overview

The behaviour of a 7-joint robot arm can be simplified with a simple double pendulum hanging down from the top. It is simple because it uses lumped masses for the dynamics, which means the entire mass of each pendulum is concentrated or "lumped" at a single point, at the end of the pendulum.

Setup

A DP is parameterised with just 2 angles, $\theta_1, \theta_2$. The desired contact is to be made at the end point or the end effector. Each joint is actuated using a motor and therefore torques are produced at both the joints.

Aim

For a distributed mass, the standard manipulator equation of motion is given by (contact forces will be added in later): $$\begin{equation} M(q)\ddot{q} + C(q,\dot{q}) = \tau_g(q) + u \end{equation}$$ $$ q=\begin{bmatrix} \theta_1\\\theta_2 \end{bmatrix}, u=\begin{bmatrix} \tau_1\\\tau_2 \end{bmatrix} $$ For lumped masses, the terms come out particularly easy (derived in the notes).

The goal is to figure out what joint torques to apply to achieve some force control or to program the interaction at the contact point.
