Simple top-down double pendulum

Overview

A simple double pendulum hanging down from the top is simple because it uses lumped masses for the dynamics, which means the entire mass of each pendulum is concentrated or "lumped" at a single point, at the end of the pendulum.

Setup

A DP is parameterised with just 2 angles, $\theta_1, \theta_2$. The desired contact is to be made at the end point or the end effector. Each joint is actuated using a motor and therefore torques are produced at both the joints.

Dynamics

For a distributed mass, the standard manipulator equation of motion is given by: $$\begin{equation} \label{eq:manipulatorEq} \underbrace{M(q)}_{\text{Inertia} \atop \text{Matrix}}\ddot{q} + \underbrace{C(q,\dot{q})}_{\scriptsize\text{Coriolis}} = \underbrace{\tau_g(q)}_{\scriptsize\text{Gravity}} + \underbrace{u}_{\text{Joint} \atop \text{Torques}} + \underbrace{\tau_{ext}}_{\text{Contact}} \end{equation}$$ $$ q=\begin{bmatrix} \theta_1\\\theta_2 \end{bmatrix}, u=\begin{bmatrix} \tau_1\\\tau_2 \end{bmatrix} $$ For lumped masses, the terms come out particularly easy (derived in the notes).

Control techniques

  • Feedback cancelling control
  • Joint stiffness control implemented using feedback cancellation
