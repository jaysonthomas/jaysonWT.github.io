Setup Description

Two-link arm: Forward Kinematics

Setup

A simple double pendulum hanging down from the top is simple because it uses lumped masses for the dynamics, which means the entire mass of each pendulum is concentrated or "lumped" at a single point, at the end of the pendulum.
A DP is parameterised with just 2 angles, $\theta_1, \theta_2$. The desired contact is to be made at the end point or the end effector. Each joint is actuated using a motor and therefore torques are produced at both the joints.

Description

The end-effector position is given as: $$\begin{align*} x &= f_x(l_1, l_2, \theta_1, \theta_2) \\ &= l_1\,\cos(\theta_1) + l_2\,\cos(\theta_1+\theta_2) \\ y &= f_y(l_1, l_2, \theta_1, \theta_2) \\ &= l_1\,\sin(\theta_1) + l_2\,\sin(\theta_1+\theta_2) \end{align*}$$
