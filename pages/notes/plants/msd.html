Dynamics of a mass spring damper system

FBD analysis

At equilibrium, there is no horizontal force acting on the spring. A spring force $F_s$ acts in the oppposite direction to the direction of displacement from the equilibrium point and is proportional to it. For a positive displacment, the spring expands and the spring force tends to bring the object to its equilibrium position. $$ F_s = -k_sd_{em} $$ The damper dampens any mechanical oscillations. It is proportional to and acts opposite to the direction of movement. $$ F_d = -k_d\dot{d}_{em} $$ The dynamics is governed by Newton's second law: $$\begin{align} &ma = \sum_{i=1}^{n}F_i \nonumber \\ &m\ddot{d}_{em} = F - k_d\dot{d}_{em} - k_sd_{em} \nonumber \\ \label{eq:msd} &m\ddot{d}_{em} + k_d\dot{d}_{em} + k_sd_{em} = F \end{align}$$ The equation is a second-order, linear ODE:
  • Linear because $d_{em}, \dot{d}_{em}, \ddot{d}_{em}$ appear as linear functions
  • Second order because it is the order of the highest derivative

Solution

The analytical solution to equation \ref{eq:msd} is given by: $$ d_{em} = d_{em}(t, d_{em}(0), \dot{d}_{em}(0), F(t)) $$ $d_{em}(0), \dot{d}_{em}(0)$ are the initial position and velocity. The goal is to numerically solve \ref{eq:msd} instead of using the analytical form.

State space model

To arrive at a numerical solution, the system is written as a series of first-order ODEs, defined collectively as a state space model of the system. More generally, an n-th order ODE can be written as a system of n first-order ODEs.

State variables

$$\begin{align*} x_1 &= d_{em} \\ x_2 &= \dot{d}_{em} \end{align*}$$

State propagation

Differentiating the equations for the state variables: $$\begin{align*} \dot{x}_1 &= \dot{d}_{em} = x_2 \\ \dot{x}_2 &= \ddot{d}_{em} \\ &= -\frac{k_d}{m} \dot{d}_{em} - \frac{k_s}{m}d_{em} + \frac{1}{m}F \\ &= -\frac{k_d}{m} x_2 - \frac{k_s}{m}x_1 + \frac{1}{m}F \end{align*}$$ In vector form: $$ \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} \dot{x}_1 \\ \dot{x}_2 \end{bmatrix}}_{\dot{x}} = \underbrace{ \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -\frac{k_s}{m} & -\frac{k_d}{m} \end{bmatrix}}_{A} \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{bmatrix}}_{x} + \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ \frac{1}{m}\end{bmatrix}}_{B} \underbrace{F}_{u} $$ $u$ is the control input, $A,B$ are system matrices and $x$ is the state vector.

System output

The output equation maps the state vector into the output vector (here, a scalar). A system output is a system variable or a function of system variables whose dynamical behavior we want to track (using sensors). The output equation: $$ y = \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0\end{bmatrix}}_{C} x $$ The state $x_1$ is tracked, i.e. the displacement from the equilibrium position.
